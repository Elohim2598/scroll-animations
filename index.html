Each particle traces a unique spiral trajectory from a random point in space, pulled inward by a shared gravitational centre. As you scroll, the animation timeline advances, gathering, compressing, then releasing in a burst of simulated light.

No libraries. No WebGL. Everything is drawn with the native Canvas 2D API using polar coordinate math, additive compositing, and motion-trail rendering to simulate plasma physics entirely in software.

01, Techniques
Technique 01

Spiral Physics

Each particle stores its polar origin, radius and angle from centre. The scroll progress drives both the shrinking radius and the cumulative rotation angle, tracing a true Archimedean spiral inward without requiring a physics engine.

Technique 02

Additive Blending

Composite mode is set to lighter, each particle adds its luminance to whatever sits beneath. Where hundreds of paths converge at the core, light values stack to create a self-illuminating plasma centre without any explicit glow shader.

Technique 03

Scroll Choreography

There is no time-based playback. The entire animation timeline is a pure function of scroll position, scrub forward to collapse the star, scrub back to reverse the infall. The phase transitions between spiral, singularity, and supernova are deterministic.

A different primitive, a different logic. Instead of point particles, this study renders overlapping sinusoidal wave fronts, 55 horizontal lines, each oscillating at two frequencies. As scroll advances, the frequencies diverge, pushing the system through emergence, resonance, and peak complexity.

02, Techniques
Technique 01

Polyline Primitives

Each of the 55 wave lines is a single continuous Path2D drawn in 3 px increments across the full canvas width. The rendering primitive is a stroke, not a filled arc, an intentional contrast to the first study's particle dots that changes the entire visual grammar.

Technique 02

Frequency Divergence

Two base frequencies, f1 and f2, share a ratio that drifts from 1.5× to 3.7× as scroll advances. When they are close in frequency, long-period beats form across the canvas. As the ratio grows, the interference becomes aperiodic and the pattern breaks into apparent chaos, a third harmonic f3 emerges only at peak resonance.

Technique 03

Antinode Highlighting

Each row samples the combined wave sum at its horizontal midpoint. Where the two waves are in phase, a constructive antinode, the sum exceeds a threshold and a second, narrower highlight stroke is drawn on top at higher opacity. Destructive nodes stay dark. The result is a self-organising brightness map that follows the physics of real wave interference rather than being painted manually.
